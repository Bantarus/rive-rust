### Dozen (no interlock) atomics fallback — a pre-existing rive-internal finding on HEAVY content
  cloner   : PLS=Atomics  SYNC-HAZARDs=0   interop VUIDs=0
  eye      : PLS=Atomics  SYNC-HAZARDs=0   interop VUIDs=0
  bigwheel : PLS=Atomics  SYNC-HAZARDs=240 interop VUIDs=0   <-- FINDING
  The hazard is SYNC-HAZARD-READ-AFTER-WRITE on "atomic coverage backing" (rive's OWN internal atomic-PLS
  coverage STORAGE_IMAGE, binding #3) — SHADER_STORAGE_READ after SHADER_STORAGE_WRITE across draws,
  write_barriers:0. It is rive's atomic-fallback algorithm, NOT our zero-copy interop (our shared VkImage,
  the COLOR->SHADER_READ barrier, and the timeline semaphore are uninvolved).
  Root cause (characterized): triggered ONLY on Dozen's D3D12-translated atomic execution + heavy
  overlapping fills. On the native 4090 it is ABSENT in every config — clockwise, RIVE_NO_CLOCKWISE
  (atomics, interlock-enabled device), AND RIVE_FORCE_ATOMIC (atomics, rive told no-interlock): all 0
  hazards. So it is not on the shipped clockwise default, not on any native path, and not introduced by
  M2c (atomic-path code unchanged). Follow-up: rive-upstream atomic-PLS barriering + testing on real
  (conformant) no-interlock hardware, which is unavailable here (Dozen is the only no-interlock device).
  (rc=1 on the Dozen cells is a validation-error-on-exit artifact of the known Dozen cubemap VUID; renders
  completed and captured correctly.)
